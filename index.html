Buddhism

Buddhism is an ancient religion that began in Northeastern India more than 2,500 years ago. The original Buddha, Siddhartha Gautama, was born a prince before he cast his riches aside to reach Nibbāna (Nirvana). In life he was a spiritual teacher and meditator who renounced the lavish lifestyle he inherited and travelled as a normal man. Buddhism is now the fourth largest religion in the world with over 300 million people currently practicing its teachings. Modern Buddhism is a collection of teachings that were compiled by the Buddhist community and passed down through the centuries. these teachings include ‘The Five Precepts’ which will be seen as we take a step towards learning the history of a renounced prince who found his calling.

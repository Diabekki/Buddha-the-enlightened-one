Buddhism

Buddhism is an ancient religion that began in North-eastern India more than 2,500 years ago and was founded by Siddhartha Gautama who was as born a prince before he cast his riches aside to reach Nibbāna (Nirvana). Buddhism has reached many countries from its origins and is now the fourth largest religion in the world with over 300 million people currently practicing its teachings. Buddha itself means "enlightened" and Buddhists do not worship a god or deity, they spend their lives trying to find nirvana and inner peace through meditation practices. Buddhism believes heavy in reincarnation and the consequences of Karma, if an individual behaves in a way to hurt any living being, it will harm their chances of a good rebirth. Modern Buddhism is a collection of teachings that were compiled by the Buddhist community and passed down through the centuries. these teachings include ‘The Five Precepts’ which will be seen as we take a step towards learning the history of a renounced prince who found his calling.
